Frocasting GAV Score | MOSPI GOI

Presentation Overview
Code Overview

Identify the relevant industries for which the GAV score will be calculated, considering factors such as market size, availability of data, and strategic importance. Gather historical data for each industry, including financial statements, industry-specific metrics, and any other relevant data sources. Preprocess the time series data, ensuring data quality, consistency, and compatibility across industries. Conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand the patterns, trends, and seasonality present in the data. Select appropriate time series forecasting models based on the characteristics of the data and industry dynamics, such as ARIMA, exponential smoothing, or state-space models. Split the data into training and testing sets, using a suitable time period to validate the accuracy of the forecasting models. Train the selected models using the historical data, adjusting model parameters and configurations as needed. Apply the trained models to forecast the GAV scores for each industry, using future time periods. Evaluate the accuracy of the forecasting models by comparing the predicted GAV scores with the actual values from the testing set. Calculate the GAV scores for each industry based on the forecasted values, considering the specific formula or methodology defined for the project. Analyze the calculated GAV scores across industries to identify trends, outliers, and industry-specific insights. Visualize the GAV scores and associated metrics using appropriate charts, graphs, and dashboards for effective communication and decision-making. Conduct sensitivity analysis to understand the impact of different factors on the GAV scores, such as changes in market conditions, regulatory environment, or industry-specific events.
